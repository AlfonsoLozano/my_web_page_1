Fundamentals of Computer Engineering

Content

This subject is structured into two main parts: theoretical and practical.

The theoretical section begins with an exploration of the historical background of Computer Engineering, examining its evolution from the past to the present and projecting its future. It emphasizes Computer Engineering as both an academic discipline and a professional field. Additionally, it covers the representation and organization of information, introducing essential concepts such as number systems, data coding, and models. Contemporary topics like Big Data, data protection, and the legal and ethical challenges of information management are also discussed. Furthermore, the structure and functionality of computer systems are examined, including hardware, software, operating systems, and networks. Modern paradigms and technologies such as cloud computing, quantum computing, artificial intelligence, blockchain, IoT, robotics, and smart cities are explored, highlighting their interconnected roles in today's computing landscape.

In the practical component, students gain experience in web development. They learn to create web pages using HTML5, manipulate the Document Object Model (DOM), and apply CSS3 for design and layout. These projects provide a hands-on approach to applying theoretical knowledge in a practical setting

Objectives

  • Embrace the importance of human-centric values in professional settings, fostering a dedication to serving users responsibly and benefiting the greater good.
  • Explore the core principles of technological advancement through a historical lens.
  • Recognize your impact on society and envision your future contributions as an Computer Engineer.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and effectively use terminology related to hardware and software when discussing computer systems' design, operation, and use.
  • Identify and analyze key historical developments in computer science, correlating them with emerging trends and future advancements.
  • Create a webpage using HTML, CSS, and GitHub Pages to develop a web system.
